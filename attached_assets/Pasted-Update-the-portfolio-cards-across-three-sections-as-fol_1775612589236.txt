Title: Joint Venture Due Diligence and B2B Pipeline Intelligence for a Global Technology Giant in Saudi Arabia
Body: This engagement supported due diligence for a global technology giant evaluating a joint venture with a major Saudi conglomerate, structured across two analytical tracks. The first track covered competitive landscaping — deconstructing incumbent and emerging technology players in the Saudi market across business model architecture, product portfolio depth, route-to-market strategies, and financial benchmarks — alongside an analysis of the prospective JV partner's conglomerate structure, revenue composition, and strategic rationale, with a focus on synergy levers and risk vectors. The second track built a proprietary B2B pipeline intelligence model through large-scale government tender scraping. 2,000+ entries were extracted from public procurement databases and structured to map the tendering landscape across: process composition and lifecycle, aggregate and segmented tender value pools, competitive capture rates by incumbent players, and forward-looking tender projections tied to Vision 2030 spending mandates. The combined output gave the client a data-driven view of the addressable B2B opportunity, competitive procurement intensity, and a prioritisation framework for pipeline targeting post-JV formation.
Key Outcomes:

Constructed a competitive intelligence matrix across Saudi market technology players, benchmarking business model architecture, financial performance, and route-to-market strategies.
Scraped, cleaned, and structured 2,000+ government procurement entries to build a proprietary tender model covering value pools, competitive capture rates, and 3-year forward projections.
Delivered a dual-track due diligence output synthesising market-entry risk, JV partner synergy assessment, and a prioritised B2B pipeline targeting framework.
